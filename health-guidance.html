How we guide you

Your health.
Your questions.

"No question is too small. No concern is too vague. If your body is worrying you, that already matters."

Women's health questions are too often dismissed with a wave or a hurried explanation. Project Nithya exists to close that gap — offering thoughtful, plain-language guidance for women across India and Sri Lanka, from people who take your concerns seriously.

Our specialty is haematology — the study of blood and blood disorders — but we also help women navigate reproductive health, hospital systems, and the preventive screenings that too often go unmentioned. We are not a replacement for medical care. We are the bridge that helps you access it.

Our specialty

When the issue
is in the blood.

Haematology — the study of blood and blood disorders — sits at the heart of women's health across our region. Anaemia affects more than half of women in India. Thalassemia carriers number in the millions across Sri Lanka. Iron deficiency, clotting disorders, postpartum haemorrhage — these are not rare conditions. They are everyday realities, and too often invisible until they become dangerous.

Project Nithya focuses here. We help women read their blood reports, navigate haematology consultations, understand carrier screening, and find the right specialists across India and Sri Lanka.

Know your healthcare rights

Right to informed consent

No procedure should happen without your understanding and agreement. You may ask questions and refuse treatment.

Right to a second opinion

You are always entitled to seek another doctor's view — especially before major procedures or surgery.

Right to your medical records

Hospitals must provide you copies of your own records, test results, and discharge summaries on request.

Right to free emergency care

Across India and Sri Lanka, hospitals cannot deny emergency treatment due to inability to pay. Know your rights — and call us if you need help asserting them.
